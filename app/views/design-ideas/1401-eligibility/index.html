  • 22 October 2020

    #4 Telling users we check

    {{ govukDetails({ summaryText: "Gareth’s hypothesis", html: '

    We’ve seen people apply to get Jobseeker’s allowance without checking their NI contributions;

    so by telling them that we do a check, we’ll prompt users to check and;

    we’ll see fewer applications rejected on the basis of NI contributions.

    ' }) }}

    Outcome

    • Users tended to miss the "we'll check" line
    • Uncertain if we want to encourage users who the "we'll check" message gives confidence to apply to
  • 21 October 2020

    #3 Removing doubt and self serving

    {{ govukDetails({ summaryText: "Gareth’s hypotheses", html: '

    We’ve seen people being told they are not eligible to get Jobseeker’s allowance after applying;

    so if we provide clear information when we know they are or aren’t eligible;

    we’ll see fewer applications rejected.

    We’ve seen people wrongly saying they’ve made enough National Insurance contributions to get Jobseeker’s allowance;

    so if we provide clear signposting to help users find this out for themselves;

    we’ll see fewer applications rejected on the basis of NI contributions.

    ' }) }}

    Outcome

    • Users like definite outcomes (you are eligible)
    • Not all users want to use the phone to call HMRC (aspergers user)
  • 9 October 2020

    #2 Provide users with information about NI contributions

    • Iteration 2.1
    • Iteration 2.0
    {{ govukDetails({ summaryText: "Dan’s hypothesis", html: '

  • 9 October 2020

    #1 Ask about employment and benefits

    {{ govukDetails({ summaryText: "Anne’s hypothesis", html: '

    We’ve seen people wrongly saying they’ve made enough National Insurance contributions to get Jobseeker’s allowance;

    so if we ask specific questions about employment and benefits - rather than about NI contributions - and route the user accordingly;

    we’ll see fewer applications rejected on the basis of NI contributions.

    ' }) }}

    Outcome

    • Users confused about the meaning of employed, some are employed and on zero hours contracts
    • Users confused by "most and some of the time"
    • Not all users know what the other benefits are
